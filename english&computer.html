Comprehension of Unseen Passage

An unseen passage is given followed by 5 questions. You must read carefully and answer from the passage only.

Reading Strategy Types of Questions Finding Main Idea Inference Questions Vocabulary in Context
1.1

What is an Unseen Passage?

Unseen Passage: A reading passage that you have NOT studied before the exam. You read it during the exam and answer questions based only on what the passage says.Unseen passage ma answer PASSAGE MA THI j aape — apni general knowledge thi nahi. Passage dhyan thi vaacho! Your own general knowledge is secondary — the answer must be from the passage.

In DDCET, you get 1 passage followed by 5 MCQ questions. The passage is usually 150–300 words about a general topic (nature, science, communication, society, etc.).

1.2

Step-by-Step Reading Strategy

  1. Read the questions first (30 seconds) — Before reading the passage, skim the 5 questions. This tells you what to look for.
  2. Read the passage once fully — Don't stop at difficult words. Get the overall meaning and topic.
  3. Read again and underline key information — Focus on names, dates, causes, effects, and definitions.
  4. Answer each question — Go back to the passage to find the answer. Do NOT answer from memory or general knowledge.
  5. Check your answers — Verify each answer against the passage text.
1.3

Types of Questions and How to Answer

Question TypeHow to IdentifyStrategy
Factual / Direct"According to the passage…", "What does the passage say about…"Find the exact sentence in the passage with the answer. The answer is directly stated.
Main Idea / Title"What is the best title for this passage?", "The passage is mainly about…"The main idea covers the WHOLE passage, not just one part. Ask: what is the author's central point?
Inference / Implied"We can conclude that…", "It can be inferred that…", "The author suggests…"The answer is not directly stated but implied. Use clues from the text to reason.
Vocabulary in Context"The word '___' in the passage means…"Read the sentence around the word. Choose the meaning that fits best in that context.
True / False / Not Given"Which of the following is true/false according to the passage?"Check each option against the passage carefully. "Not Given" means the passage doesn't mention it.
1.4

Tips for Scoring Full Marks

  • Never guess without reading — All answers are in the passage.
  • Beware of "close but wrong" options — Examiners include options that sound correct but have one wrong word.
  • For vocabulary questions — The correct answer is a synonym that makes sense in that exact sentence.
  • For main idea questions — Avoid options that are too specific (only one paragraph) or too broad (beyond the passage).
  • Stay focused on the passage — Even if you know more about the topic, only what the passage says counts.
  • Avoid extreme options — Options with words like "always", "never", "all", "none" are usually wrong unless the passage clearly states so.
Practice Tip: Read one newspaper article or short story every day. This trains your brain to quickly understand passages and find key information.
1.5

Sample Passage + Questions

Sample Passage

"Communication is a fundamental human need. It allows people to share thoughts, express feelings, and coordinate actions. Humans communicate not only through words but also through gestures, facial expressions, and body language. Even silence can be a form of communication. When we listen carefully to another person, we show respect and build stronger relationships. Good communication skills are essential for success in both personal and professional life."

Q1. What is the passage mainly about?
→ (A) The importance of communication — ✓ CORRECT (covers whole passage)

Q2. According to the passage, how do humans communicate?
→ (D) Through words, gestures, facial expressions, body language, and silence — ✓ CORRECT

Q3. The word 'fundamental' in the passage most likely means:
→ (B) Basic and essential — ✓ CORRECT (context: "fundamental human need")

Q4. Which of the following can be inferred from the passage?
→ (C) Listening is as important as speaking — ✓ CORRECT (implied by "listen carefully = show respect")

Q5. The passage suggests good communication is important for:
→ (D) Both personal and professional life — ✓ CORRECT (last sentence)

Theory of Communication

Understanding how communication works, its types, and what blocks it. This chapter is directly tested with MCQs and fill-in-the-blanks.

Process of Communication Verbal Communication Non-Verbal Communication Barriers to Communication
2.1

What is Communication?

Communication: A branch of knowledge dealing with the principles and methods by which information is conveyed. It is the process of transferring information from the sender to the recipient.
Communication is an integral instinct present in all living beings. Every organism — humans, animals, plants — communicates in some way.

Why Do We Communicate?

  • To express our feelings, thoughts, and desires
  • To pass on information to others
  • To share knowledge and ideas
  • Communication is a dreadful need of survival — without it, we cannot express our desires.
If you have no power of communication, you cannot express your desire — this is the most important loss.

Communication in Nature

EntityHow they communicate
PlantsThrough visible changes in the colour of leaves, and the falling of leaves and flowers
AnimalsBy sounds and several movements — to indicate they are hungry, unwell, or need medical attention
Very young childrenBy crying
Parents showing angerBy not speaking for some times (silent treatment)
2.2

Process of Communication

Communication Process

Key Elements of Communication

ElementDefinition

2.3

Types of Communication: Verbal vs Non-Verbal

Verbal Communication

  • Uses words — spoken or written
  • Oral: face-to-face conversation, phone calls, speeches, lectures
  • Written: letters, emails, reports, text messages
  • Precise and can be documented
  • Example: "Please come at 10 AM"

Non-Verbal Communication

  • Does NOT use words — uses body language, signals
  • Gesture: raising eyebrow, waving to a friend, nodding
  • Facial expression: smile, frown, surprise
  • Body language: posture, eye contact, distance
  • Often more powerful than words — conveys emotion instantly
A gesture like raising an eyebrow or waving to a friend can be used as a means to communicate — this is a key fact in DDCET questions.
2.4

Barriers to Communication

Barriers: Factors that block or interfere with the message that someone is trying to send, preventing effective communication.
Type of BarrierExamples & Explanation
Physical BarriersDistance, noise, poor lighting, faulty equipment
Language BarriersUsing unfamiliar technical terms or over-complicated language that cannot be understood well. Also: difference in dialects of different regions, unfamiliar accents.
Physiological BarriersIf the receiver has hearing problems, or the speaker has speech disabilities, then communication will not be effective.
Psychological BarriersStress, emotions, prejudice, lack of interest — distort how message is sent or received
Cultural BarriersDifferent customs, values, and assumptions between sender and receiver
Semantic BarriersSame word meaning different things to different people; jargon; ambiguous language

Key DDCET Facts on Barriers

  • Unfamiliar accents can make it difficult to understand the message sent.
  • The difference in dialects of different regions can also be a barrier.
  • Barriers block or interfere with the message that someone is trying to send.
  • If the receiver has hearing problems → communication will not be effective.
  • Using unfamiliar terms or over-complicated technical terms → message cannot be understood well.

How to Overcome Barriers

  • Use simple, clear language
  • Give and seek feedback
  • Be a good listener — active listening
  • Choose appropriate channel for the message
  • Be aware of cultural differences
  • Minimize physical distractions and noise

3.3

Types of Business Letters

1. Inquiry Letter

Purpose: To ask for information about products, services, prices, availability, or terms.
Key phrases: "We would like to inquire about…", "Please provide us with details regarding…", "Kindly send us your latest catalogue and price list…"

2. Reply to Inquiry Letter

Purpose: To respond to an inquiry with the requested information.
Key phrases: "Thank you for your inquiry dated…", "We are pleased to inform you that…", "Please find enclosed our catalogue/price list…"

3. Order Letter

Purpose: To officially place an order for goods or services. Should include quantity, product name, price, delivery terms, and payment method.
Key phrases: "Please supply us with the following…", "Please arrange delivery to our address by…", "Payment will be made by DD/NEFT within 30 days."

4. Complaint Letter

Purpose: To formally express dissatisfaction about a product, service, or situation and request a remedy.
Key phrases: "We regret to inform you that…", "The goods received were damaged/wrong/incomplete…", "We request you to replace/refund/rectify…", "We expect a prompt response."

5. Adjustment Letter

Purpose: Written in response to a complaint letter. It accepts the complaint and offers a solution (replacement, refund, apology).
Key phrases: "We sincerely apologize for the inconvenience caused…", "We are taking immediate steps to rectify…", "A replacement will be dispatched within 3 working days."

4.2

Tenses

Tenses show when an action takes place. There are 3 main tenses × 4 aspects = 12 tenses in English.

TenseStructureExample
Simple PresentSubject + V1 (s/es for he/she/it)She reads every day.
Present Continuousam/is/are + V-ingShe is reading now.
Present Perfecthas/have + V3 (past participle)She has read the book.
Present Perfect Continuoushas/have been + V-ingShe has been reading for 2 hours.
Simple PastSubject + V2 (past form)She read the book yesterday.
Past Continuouswas/were + V-ingShe was reading when I called.
Past Perfecthad + V3She had read the book before the exam.
Past Perfect Continuoushad been + V-ingShe had been reading for 2 hours when I arrived.
Simple Futurewill/shall + V1She will read tomorrow.
Future Continuouswill be + V-ingShe will be reading at 8 PM.
Future Perfectwill have + V3She will have read by then.
Future Perfect Continuouswill have been + V-ingShe will have been reading for 3 hours.
Signal words: Present perfect → "since, for, already, just, yet, ever, never". Past perfect → "before, after, by the time, already, when". Simple past → "yesterday, last week, ago, in 1990".
4.3

Subject-Verb Agreement

The verb must agree with its subject in number (singular/plural) and person (1st/2nd/3rd).

RuleCorrectWrong
Singular subject → singular verb (add s/es)He reads books.He read books. ✗
Plural subject → plural verb (no s/es)They read books.They reads books. ✗
I and You take plural verbI write. You write.I writes. ✗
Two subjects joined by 'and' → pluralRam and Shyam are here.Ram and Shyam is here. ✗
Either/Neither/Each/Every/None → singularEach student has a book.Each student have ✗
Collective nouns (team, class, jury) → singular in IndiaThe team has won.The team have won. ✗
Subject joined by "or/nor" → verb agrees with nearest subjectNeither he nor they are ready.Neither he nor they is ready. ✗
Indefinite pronouns (everyone, nobody, someone) → singularEveryone needs food.Everyone need food. ✗

Grammar Quick Rules
Grammar na rules fast yaad karva maate aa shortcuts use karo — exam ma time bachshe!
  • Subject pronoun (I/he/she/we/they) → used as subject | Object pronoun (me/him/her/us/them) → after verbs/prepositions
  • Myself, yourself etc. → only used when subject and object are same person
  • Present perfect: have/has + V3 (not "I have went" → WRONG; "I have gone" → correct)
  • Conditional: "If I had known… I would have gone" (NOT "would of" — that's WRONG)
  • their = possession | there = place | they're = they are
  • its = possession | it's = it is
  • your = possession | you're = you are

Sentence Correction — Practice Questions & Answers

1. Choose grammatically correct: "It's been three weeks since we last ___ him."
✓ CORRECT: "It's been three weeks since we last saw him."
Reason: Use simple past (saw) after "since" with present perfect (it's been).
2. Pick grammatically correct sentence about the dog
✓ CORRECT: "Their dog ran away while they were in the park."
Reason: "Their" = possession. "Were" = past of "be" (they were in the park).
3. Incorrect punctuation: coffee question
✓ INCORRECT: "Can you please pick me up a coffee, while you are out."
Reason: A question must end with (?) not a period (.). Should be: "Can you please pick me up a coffee while you are out?"
4. "He ____ the train to get to work." — Choose correct
✓ CORRECT: "He took the train to get to work."
Reason: Use simple past (took) for a habitual past action. "He takes" is also possible for present habit. "He take" ✗ (missing s). "He taking" ✗ (no auxiliary verb).
5. Grammatically incorrect sentence about "best"
✓ INCORRECT: "My car ran more better than hers."
Reason: "more better" is a double comparative — WRONG. Should be just "better".
6. "If I had known about the party, I would have ___"
✓ CORRECT: "If I had known about the party, I would have gone to it."
Reason: Conditional type 3 = "If + past perfect, would have + past participle". NEVER "would of" — that is wrong.
7. Apostrophe error: The cat's ran away
✓ INCORRECT: "The cat's ran away when the dog's arrived."
Reason: "cat's" and "dog's" are possessive forms — wrongly used here. Should be "cats ran away when the dogs arrived." (plural, no apostrophe needed for simple plural).
8. "Me and my brother" vs "My brother and I"
✓ INCORRECT: "Me and my brother decided to get our parents a gift."
✓ CORRECT: "My brother and I decided to get our parents a gift."
Reason: "I" is a subject pronoun. "Me" is an object pronoun — cannot be the subject of a verb.
9. Dangling modifier: "at eight years old"
✓ INCORRECT: "My father decided to give me a pony for Christmas, at eight years old."
Reason: The phrase "at eight years old" appears to modify "father" (making him eight years old) — but the intended meaning is that I was eight. This is a dangling modifier. Correct: "At eight years old, I received a pony from my father for Christmas."
